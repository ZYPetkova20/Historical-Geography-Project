THE HISTORY OF

THE GREATEST GEOGRAPHY DISCOVERIES

AMERICA

12 October 1492 marks an event which was to change life on both sides of the Atlantic forever. This date represents the discovery of America by the Italian explorer and navigator Christopher Columbus.

learn more

SEA ROUTH TO INDIA

The Portuguese discovery of the sea route to India was the first recorded trip directly from Europe to India. Under the command of Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama was undertaken in 1495–1499.

learn more

FIRST CIRCUMNAVIGATION

One of the most famous Portuguese-born explorers was Fernao de Magalhaes, who instigated and organized the first circumnavigation from 1519 to 1522.

learn more

NORTH POLE

The North Pole was one of the last remaining laurels of earthly exploration, a prize for which countless explorers from many nations had suffered and died for 300 years.

learn more

SOUTH POLE

On December 15, 1911, the South Pole was discovered by Roald Amundsen, "the last of the Vikings." The race to the South Pole was one of the biggest international competitions before the race to the moon and was full of tragedy and triumph.
